Bio

Örlygur Steinar Arnalds is an Icelandic musician whose work spans everything from experimental instrumental composition, electroacoustic music, free improvisation, and club music. He gravitates towards collaboration, and while he primarily works in bands and collective projects, he occasionally works solo.

Since 2016, he has been an active member of Reykjavík's DIY/DIT music scene — organizing events, releasing music, and doing sound engineering. He is a founding member of the art collective and label post-dreifing, and has participated in organizing events and releases with RASK collective, Agalma label and Smekkleysa record store/label. In 2022 he graduated from the Iceland University of the Arts with a BA in composition and new media.

Örlygur is a member of the bands sideproject, glupsk and korter í flog. With these projects, he has released albums on acclaimed labels such as SVBKVLT, SUPERPANG and Mengi records, as well as playing festivals such as Primavera Sound, Unsound, Norberg Festival, All Ears, By:Larm, Roskilde, Nyege Nyege and local festivals Extreme Chill and Iceland Airwaves.

Drawn to the physical process of how sound is produced, he is interested in its effects, social and cultural relations, and the bodily reactions it evokes. He embraces playful, process-driven methods — favoring connection over structure and allowing the production of sound itself to shape its form.

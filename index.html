About

Örlygur Steinar Arnalds is an Icelandic musician whose work spans everything from experimental instrumental composition, electroacoustic music, free improvisation, and club music. He gravitates towards collaboration, and while he primarily works in bands and collective projects, he occasionally works solo.

For the past eight years, he has been an active member of Reykjavík's DIY/DIT music scene—organizing events, releasing music, and doing sound engineering. He is a founding member of the art collective and label post-dreifing, and has participated in organizing events and releases with RASK collective, Agalma label and Smekkleysa record store/label. In 2022 he graduated from the Iceland University of the Arts with a BA in composition and new media.

Örlygur is a member of the bands sideproject, glupsk and korter í flog. With these projects, he has released albums on acclaimed labels such as SVBKVLT, SUPERPANG and Mengi records, as well as playing festivals such as Primavera Sound, Unsound, Norberg Festival, All Ears, By:Larm, Roskilde, Nyege Nyege and local festivals Extreme Chill and Iceland Airwaves.

Drawn to the physical process of how sound is produced, he is interested in its effects, social and cultural relations, and the bodily reactions it evokes. He embraces playful, process-driven methods—favoring connection over structure and allowing the production of sound itself to shape its form.

Solo Compositions

r lego

For MIDI-controlled organ · 7 February 2026, Hallgrímskirkja, Reykjavík · ca. 4'

In 2025 I got asked to do a midi piece for the Hallgrímskirkja organ. I got to do a few experiments in the church and as usual I got really frustrated—it all, for lack of a better description, sounded like an organ. I tried to get some inspiration from Ligeti's organ pieces but figured out a lot of the beautiful textures he achieves stem from the fact that he had control of the wind pressure, which I had no control of.

I made a few generators in Max focusing on the highest and lowest registers of the organ, using only 2 stops—mostly stops closest to sine waves. The length of the notes was also, most of the time, not much longer than 20ms.

I had one object generate clouds of notes through a chaotic process and another generating clusters with semi-stable rhythm. I created parameters for the density of the process, speed, and how large a range of notes to pull from, as well as where on the register. I also had control of the stops in/out messages, so I could use these same processes on the stops, which created faint mechanical wooden sounds. With these rapid, super-short tones I managed to get to a point where I was fairly happy with the sound.

stykki1

For electric guitar, cello, and piano · Commissioned by K!art ensamble · 13 August 2022, Mengi, Reykjavík · ca. 6–12'

In 2022 I made a piece for the Danish ensemble K!ART, performed in Mengi. The piece was an attempt to make a modular score. I made a lot of experiments on my guitar and a piano I had access to. I always like sounds where the production is not easily recognized, so with instrumental music I have often been frustrated by distinct instrumental sound and I try my best to find new ways of instrumental sound production.

For this I tuned all the guitar strings so low that you can barely hear a stable pitch. From that I found a lot of textures that excited me. For the piano I just put Blu-tack on all the strings to dampen the traditional overtones and tried to distribute it diagonally for overtone variations.

From these experiments I created sound blocks and notated them similar to some scores by Lachenmann. I left the speed of the blocks open, as well as allowing the instrumentalists to start wherever in the block, with freedom to read it forward and backwards. I also left it up to the players to move between blocks freely. Ideally the players should study the blocks and remember them to go smoothly from one to the other—creating textures I did not imagine.

Collaborations

Rímnadans

Participatory performance work by the Icelandic composer collective S.L.Á.T.U.R., inspired by traditional Icelandic rímur chanting and dance practices. Premiered at Harpa during Dark Music Days 2024.

Phygital Glitch

Music and sound for the performance work by choreographer and artist Gígja Jónsdóttir, created in collaboration with graduating students of the contemporary dance department of the Iceland University of the Arts.

Oneirokritikon

Music by sideproject for the short film Oneirokritikon by visual artist Arna Beth, exhibited at Hafnarborg in the exhibition "Landscape for the Chosen One."

Töfrafundur áratug síðar

Performance work by Libia Castro & Ólafur Ólafsson. Musician/performer role at Reykjavík Art Museum in collaboration with Cycle Art Festival.

Sigtryggur Berg: A Compendium Of Terrestrial Disjection — Release Party

Arto Lindsay — I Had A Fever When

In 2023 Arto Lindsay came to Iceland to play in Mengi for two gigs. I ended up doing sound on one of the gigs. For the trip he also wanted to make an album which I helped record, and ended up contributing electronics. The session also included Skúli Sverrisson and John McCowen.
